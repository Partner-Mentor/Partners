Chapter A — Enrollment Basics

Take
the first
step.

Nine questions

The Microsoft AI Cloud Partner Program — formerly MPN — is the global membership through which every partner relationship begins. Here is how enrollment actually works.

01
What is the Microsoft Cloud Partner Program?
+

Microsoft's official partner membership program — formerly the Microsoft Partner Network (MPN), now the Microsoft AI Cloud Partner Program. A global community of partners, offerings, and resources to build and deploy successful business solutions.

02
How do I enroll and become a Microsoft partner?
+

Visit partner.microsoft.com → "Become a Partner." Three steps:

  • Create a free account with organization info and goals.
  • Confirm company details (address, legal contact, registration number).
  • Validate credentials (email, employment).
03
How long does it take to get approved?
+

The sign-up form is quick, but verification may take a few business days to a couple of weeks. Microsoft verifies your email domain ownership and business details.

04
Do I need to sign any agreement or contract?
+

Yes — the Microsoft Partner Agreement, covering code of conduct, confidentiality, trademark usage, and policy compliance.

05
What is a "Partner ID" (formerly MPN ID)?
+

A unique identifier for your organization's partnership with Microsoft. Critical for Partner Admin Link (PAL), applying for programs, and tracking your capability score.

06
Are there program levels like "Silver" and "Gold"?
+

No. The old Silver and Gold competency levels were retired in October 2022. They were replaced with the new Solutions Partner designations and the 100-point Partner Capability Score model.

07
What is Partner Center?
+

The single destination to manage your business relationship with Microsoft — update profile, add users, track designation progress, download software, manage referrals, sign up for incentive programs, and access support.

08
Do I have to renew my membership each year?
+

Membership stays active while you remain in good standing — no annual reapplication. Paid benefits (Action Pack, Solutions Partner packages) require annual payment. Solutions Partner designations are reviewed yearly.

09
What if my company has multiple branches or subsidiaries?
+

Use a Partner Global Account (PGA) as the primary account and add each country office or subsidiary as a separate Partner Location Account (PLA) underneath.

Chapter B — Partner Types & Business Models

Six
shapes of partner.

Seven questions

You do not have to be a reseller to be a Microsoft partner. ISVs, integrators, MSPs, advisors, and CSPs all live inside the program.

01
What kinds of partners does Microsoft work with?
+
  • ISV — Builds and sells own software / SaaS on Azure, Teams, or Dynamics 365.
  • SI — Delivers customer projects without reselling licenses.
  • MSP — Provides ongoing monitoring, management, and support.
  • VAR — Resells licenses plus services (often via CSP).
  • CSP — Sells cloud subscriptions. Direct bills Microsoft; Indirect via distributor.
  • Advisory — Consulting / deployment without reselling; recognized via PAL or CPOR.
02
Do I have to sell Microsoft products to join?
+

No. Many partners only provide advisory or deployment services. Use PAL or CPOR to get credit. Associations are the sole mechanism by which points are acknowledged.

03
Microsoft partner vs. Cloud Solution Provider (CSP)?
+

"Microsoft partner" is broad — any organization in the program. A CSP specifically resells Microsoft cloud subscriptions (Azure, M365, Dynamics 365) directly or via a distributor. All CSPs are partners, but not all partners are CSPs.

04
We're an ISV. Is the program relevant to us?
+

Yes. Access technical resources, training, and support. List applications on the Microsoft commercial marketplace. Earn a Solutions Partner designation (e.g., Digital & App Innovation if built on Azure) to demonstrate cloud expertise.

05
What if we only offer services or consulting?
+

Many Microsoft partners are services-only. Pursue the relevant Solutions Partner designation and get credit for driving usage and deployments through partner association — PAL for Azure, CPOR for M365 projects. Being "services-only" does not limit your ability to succeed.

06
What does it mean to be an "Advisory" partner?
+

Partners who advise customers without directly reselling. Use CPOR or PAL association methods so Microsoft knows you influenced those customer deployments.

07
Partner in MCPP vs. distributor or LSP?
+

A Distributor (Indirect Provider) wholesales Microsoft products to other partners — very different track, high requirements. An LSP sells volume licenses under enterprise agreements. Unless your model is recruiting other resellers, you are likely a standard partner or CSP reseller.

Chapter C — Designations & Solution Areas

One craft,
many paths.

Six questions

You don't pick a Solution Area at signup — you work toward one. Most partners earn one designation first and add more over time.

01
What is a "Solutions Partner" designation?
+

An earned credential showing proven capability and success in a specific Microsoft solution area. Six solution areas: Data & AI (Azure), Digital & App Innovation (Azure), Infrastructure (Azure), Business Applications, Modern Work, Security.

02
What happened to Gold and Silver competencies?
+

Retired in October 2022. Microsoft transitioned from the legacy Gold/Silver system to the new Solutions Partner designations based on a 100-point scoring model.

03
Is there a designation just for being a general partner?
+

No. The current program is structured around specific solution areas. If you don't yet qualify, you remain a partner member without a specific designation.

04
How do I know which Solution Area is right for us?
+
  • Infrastructure (Azure) — Migrate / manage cloud resources.
  • Digital & App Innovation — App development, modernization, DevOps.
  • Data & AI (Azure) — Analytics, data warehousing, AI/ML on Azure.
  • Business Applications — Dynamics 365 or Power Platform.
  • Modern Work — Office 365, Teams, SharePoint, device management.
  • Security — Cybersecurity (Defender, Sentinel, Entra, Purview).
05
Single area or multiple designations?
+

Free to focus on one or multiple areas. Each designation is earned independently. Many partners start with one area and earn multiple designations over time.

06
Can I change or add Solution Areas later?
+

Yes. You don't "pick" a solution area at signup — designations are something you work towards. Partner Center tracks progress separately for each area.

Chapter D — Achieving Designation

Solution
Designation —
questions answered.

The big chapter

Everything about the Partner Capability Score — how it's calculated, how it's tracked, how it's renewed, and how SMB partners qualify on a separate path.

01
How do we become a Solutions Partner?
+

Accumulate a sufficient Partner Capability Score (PCS) for your chosen solution area. Max 100 points across 3 categories. Reach at least 70 points total with no category at 0 pts.

02
Performance, Skilling, and Customer Success — what are they?
+
  • Performance — Net customer adds (new minus lost, trailing 12 months).
  • Skilling — Intermediate and Advanced certifications held by your team.
  • Customer Success — Usage growth and deployments across customer workloads.
03
How is the Partner Capability Score calculated?
+

Microsoft's 100-point measure of a partner's readiness in each solution area. Calculated automatically from Partner Center data using defined thresholds. Partners must score at least 70 points with activity in all three areas.

04
Do requirements differ across solution areas?
+

The general structure is consistent, but specific metrics and qualifying certifications differ by area. See the Scoring Matrix above for point breakdowns per area, or the Deep Dives below for step-by-step thresholds.

05
Reach 70 points — do I get the designation automatically?
+

Once you reach 70 PCS points with non-zero in every required metric, you attain Qualified status. Microsoft automatically enrolls your profile — no separate "apply" action required. You must meet the same criteria again during the annual renewal window.

06
Can we skip a category?
+

Cannot skip any category. You need at least some points in each (every metric must be greater than zero). You don't need to max out all three.

07
How can we track our Partner Capability Score?
+

Log into Partner Center → Membership section. View enrollment status, qualification status, and metric-level details for each solution area.

08
Time frame to achieve a designation?
+

Metrics measured on a rolling 12-month basis. For new partners, eligibility window includes the current month and the last five months. Most new partners take 6–18 months to achieve their first designation.

09
What are "net customer adds" in Performance?
+

New customers started working with minus any lost customers (trailing 12 months). For Azure: a customer with at least $1,000 ACR/month counts as an add. Customers with ACR below $1,000 for two straight months count against the partner.

10
What counts as customer "Usage Growth"?
+

Increasing consumption of Microsoft services. For Azure: ACR growth year-over-year (minimum $1,000 ACR). For Modern Work: active usage of M365 services. For Business Applications: Dynamics 365 monthly active usage.

11
What are "deployments" in Customer Success?
+

Specific Azure workloads or M365 features deployed for customers. For Infrastructure: needs 5 unique Azure Service Level 2 workloads (all Azure services eligible except virtual machines and VM licenses).

12
All three Azure designations or just one?
+

You can pursue any or all. Each covers different specialties. Some certifications and customer work can contribute to multiple Azure designations. Many cloud partners eventually achieve all three.

13
Can I lose a designation after achieving it?
+

Yes. Valid for one year until your anniversary/renewal date. If your PCS falls below 70 — e.g., certified employees leave or a major customer's usage declines — you won't meet renewal criteria.

14
Does one designation help with others?
+

Some overlap exists — certain certifications count toward multiple areas. However, the score itself is separate per area — an 80-point score in Modern Work doesn't give you an Azure designation.

15
Do all partners need to become Solutions Partners?
+

No. You can remain a member without a designation. However, earning a designation gives you a badge, is often a prerequisite for incentive programs and co-sell eligibility, and signals competence to customers.

16
What if we work primarily with small businesses?
+

Microsoft has separate Enterprise vs. SMB tracks in five of six solution areas. In Modern Work and Security, Microsoft evaluates you on both paths and chooses the higher score automatically.

17
Do I report performance, or is it tracked automatically?
+

Tracked automatically as long as you have proper associations (PAL, CPOR, DPOR, CSP) in place. Without associations, points are not recognized.

18
Certificate or logo on designation?
+

Yes. Digital badge / logo for each designation. Common benefits include GTM services, TP&D advisory hours, and technical support incidents. Download badge graphics from Partner Center.

19
New partner, no prior customers — how do we approach this?
+
  • Focus on Skilling first — most directly controllable category.
  • Use Microsoft Learn (free), Partner Training Center, and the Partner Skilling Hub.
  • Plan for initial customer engagements (even small POCs) and use PAL / CPOR from day one.
  • Set realistic goals: 6–12+ months; use the Partner Center dashboard to track what you need.

Chapter E — Certifications & Skilling

Eleven
questions on
certifications.

Skilling category

Certifications are directly tied to Skilling points. Without them, you cannot qualify — every category must show greater-than-zero activity.

01
Why are Microsoft certifications important?
+

Certifications are directly tied to the Skilling category of your PCS. Without certifications, you cannot earn Skilling points, and you need greater than zero in every category to qualify for a designation.

02
How do we find out which certifications we need?
+
  • Partner Center Solutions Partner Dashboard — live scores, Skilling breakdowns and gaps.
  • Microsoft Learn Partner Center Documentation — official metric definitions and certification lists per solution area.
03
How can our team prepare for exams?
+
  • Microsoft Learn — free online learning platform.
  • Instructor-Led Training — Microsoft authorized learning partners.
  • Practice Exams — from authorized providers.
04
Intermediate vs. Advanced — what is the difference?
+

Intermediate (Associate) — Role-based certifications validating mid-level skills. Advanced (Expert / Specialty) — Senior-level certifications requiring deeper proof. Most solution areas separate skilling points across the two tiers.

05
How are employee certifications linked to our company?
+

Each employee must link their Microsoft Learn profile to your Partner Center tenant via their work account. Once linked, their Microsoft Certifications count toward your Skilling score automatically.

06
Do certifications expire?
+

Most role-based Microsoft Certifications expire annually. Renewal is free via Microsoft Learn — short, online, knowledge-check style. Expired certifications stop counting toward Skilling points immediately.

07
Do we need multiple certified employees or just one?
+

Multiple unique individuals are required. Most solution areas need at least 5 unique people at the Intermediate tier and 5 at the Advanced tier on the Enterprise track.

08
Are there free certifications?
+

Microsoft offers free Fundamentals exams periodically through partner training programs. Role-based associate/expert exams are paid. Some partner benefits packages include exam vouchers.

09
If a certified employee leaves, do we lose the points?
+

Yes. If a certified individual leaves and is no longer associated, their certifications will stop counting. Try to have multiple people with required certifications.

10
Do certs count for multiple solution areas?
+

Some certifications count toward multiple areas. Azure Administrator Associate and Azure Solutions Architect Expert are prerequisites across all three Azure designations. Domain-specific certs (Dynamics 365 Functional Consultant) only count toward Business Applications.

11
Which certs count toward Skilling per area?
+

See the complete lists below — one card per solution area.

Chapter F — PAL · CPOR · DPOR

How Microsoft
sees your work.

Twelve questions

Associations are the sole mechanism by which your customer work is acknowledged. Without them, no points — regardless of how much you delivered.

01
How does Microsoft recognize our customer work?
+
  • PAL (Partner Admin Link) — For Azure plan subscriptions (modern commerce).
  • DPOR (Digital Partner of Record) — For Enterprise Agreement (EA) subscriptions. Does not transfer when customer moves to modern commerce.
  • CPOR (Claiming Partner of Record) — For Microsoft 365 and Dynamics 365 workloads. Customer must approve.
  • CSP — When you are the transacting reseller. Automatically tied to your partner ID.
02
What exactly is Partner Admin Link (PAL)?
+

PAL allows a partner's individual (who has access to a customer's Azure environment) to associate their Partner ID with that access. Once established, the customer's ACR and deployments count toward the partner's PCS. Setup requires access via: Guest user, Directory account, Service principal, or Azure Lighthouse.

03
How do we set up PAL in practice?
+
  • Partner employee must have RBAC access to customer's Azure subscription.
  • In Azure Portal → account settings → enter org's Partner ID under "Partner Admin Link."
  • Association is recorded in Microsoft's systems.
  • Verify in Partner Center within the next scoring cycle. Repeat for each team member with Azure access.
04
What is CPOR (Claiming Partner of Record)?
+

Used for Microsoft 365 and Dynamics 365 workloads. Allows a partner to claim credit for influencing a customer's adoption, even when not the transacting reseller. Eligible for: Modern Work (Enterprise + DPOR, SMB + CSP) and Security (M365 security workloads). Customer must approve.

05
When should we use CPOR vs. PAL vs. being a reseller?
+
  • PAL — Azure subscriptions under modern commerce when not the reseller.
  • CPOR — Microsoft 365 or Dynamics 365 projects where you are not the license seller.
  • CSP / Reseller — If you sold the subscription; automatically tied to your partner ID.
  • DPOR — Customers on Enterprise Agreements (EA); does NOT transfer when customer moves to modern commerce.
06
Customer bought licenses elsewhere — can we still get credit?
+

Yes. Use the appropriate association method: Azure → PAL; Microsoft 365 / Dynamics → CPOR. Microsoft's systems will recognize your influence even though billing went through another partner.

07
Does PAL or CPOR give us extra permissions?
+

No. PAL and CPOR are for recognition and tracking only. PAL does not grant access — you already need access before you can link your ID. CPOR doesn't change any permissions in the customer's tenant.

08
Will customers know if we associate via PAL or CPOR?
+

For PAL: customer may not notice unless they look at Azure portal settings. For CPOR: customer must approve the association — Microsoft sends them a notification. Always talk to the customer beforehand.

09
Multiple partners — who gets the credit?
+

Multiple associations are possible. For Azure (PAL), multiple partner IDs can be linked to the same subscription via different user accounts. Focus on ensuring your own association is in place.

10
PAL is set up but we don't see points — what's wrong?
+
  • Timing — PAL attributions may take a billing cycle; CPOR claims also need time after approval.
  • Incorrect Partner ID — Use the Associated PartnerID shown on your partner profile.
  • Scope of association — For PAL, use subscription-level access for full credit.
  • Eligible workloads — For Azure Infrastructure, all services are eligible except virtual machines and VM licenses.
11
What about GDAP?
+

GDAP (Granular Delegated Admin Privileges) is about operational access — not recognition. GDAP = operational access; PAL/CPOR = attribution and recognition.

12
Tools to track customer deployments?
+

Partner Center is your primary tool. The Reports / Insights section shows Azure consumption by customer, CPOR claim statuses, etc. Official metrics only appear once associations are in place.

Chapter G — Partner Benefits

Benefits
and incentives
are not the same.

Seven questions

Benefits are resources packaged with membership. Incentives are cash, earned through measured performance. Confusing the two is the most common misunderstanding.

01
What are "partner benefits" in the program?
+

Resources and perks Microsoft provides: Product licenses for internal use · Technical support (advisory hours and support incidents) · GTM resources (marketing materials, partner directory listing) · Training and enablement (workshops, community access).

02
What are partner incentives?
+

Financial rewards (rebates or payments) for driving sales or usage of Microsoft products. Microsoft's primary incentive vehicle is the Microsoft Commerce Incentives (MCI) program.

03
How are incentives different from benefits?
+

Benefits = resources, tools, licenses, support provided as part of your membership. Pre-packaged.
Incentives = cash payouts, rebates earned by performing eligible activities. Based on measured performance.

04
What is the Microsoft Action Pack?
+

The Microsoft Action Pack Subscription (MAPS) — an entry-level paid offering: internal use licenses (e.g., M365 seats, some Azure credit), a limited number of partner support incidents, and access to training and marketing resources.

05
What are the new "partner benefits packages"?
+

Microsoft introduced tiered benefits packages ranging from entry-level (Action Pack equivalent) to more advanced packages for Solutions Partners. Three cost-saving packages are available on the Partner Center page.

06
Do we get benefits immediately after joining?
+

With just a free membership: Access to Partner Center tools and community · Access to the Training Center · Ability to buy an Action Pack · Eligibility to pursue designations. You won't automatically get internal use licenses or credits for free.

07
What benefits do we get once we become a Solutions Partner?
+

Enhanced benefits (2026): $10,000/year Azure bulk production credits · 25 Visual Studio Enterprise IDE subscriptions · GitHub Copilot Enterprise $5,000/year · Microsoft Dragon Copilot 20 users · Partner Marketing Center Pro (Unlimited) · 50 partner cloud support incidents · 20 Microsoft Product Support incidents · GTM services · TP&D advisory hours.

Chapter H — Specializations

Ten
questions on
going deeper.

Above designation

Specializations sit one tier above Solutions Partner designations — proof of deep, audited expertise in a narrower domain. Here is how they actually work.

01
Difference between a Solutions Partner designation and a Specialization?
+

A designation is the foundational achievement in a broad solution area. A Specialization is a step above — demonstrating deep, specific expertise in a narrower domain. Prerequisite: must already hold the relevant Solutions Partner designation. No separate cost. Many specializations require passing an audit.

02
How many specializations does Microsoft offer?
+

34 in total, grouped by Solution Area family:

  • Azure / CAIP (15) — AI Platform, Analytics, AI Apps, Data Warehouse Migration, Agentic DevOps, Hybrid Cloud HCI, Infra & DB Migration, Kubernetes, Azure Virtual Desktop, VMware Solution, Migrate Enterprise Apps, Networking Services, SAP on Azure, Digital Sovereignty (preview), Frontier Partner (preview).
  • Modern Work + Business Apps / AIBS (15) — Adoption & Change Management, Calling for Teams, Custom Solutions for Teams, Meetings & Meeting Rooms, Secure AI Productivity, Modernize Endpoints, Microsoft Copilot, Low Code App Dev, SMB Management, Business Intelligence, Finance, Intelligent Automation, Sales, Service, Supply Chain.
  • Security (4) — Cloud Security, Identity & Access Management, Data Security, Threat Protection.
03
What are the requirements to achieve a Specialization?
+

For Azure specializations: meet the qualification requirements (aligned Solutions Partner designation, Performance and Skilling requirements), then schedule and pass the audit. For Modern Work and Security: a different enrollment path applies. Several Security specializations are moving to an audit-based model in coming months per the May 2026 announcement.

04
How do we prepare for a specialization audit?
+
  • Review the requirements document in detail — the Partner Center Specializations FAQ is the source of truth.
  • Organize documentation: architecture diagrams, project scopes, customer attestations.
  • Perform a self-assessment or dry run.
  • Ensure your team is ready — auditors may interview technical team members.
05
Do specializations require an on-site audit or is it remote?
+

In most cases, audits are done remotely by third-party auditors — they schedule calls to review documentation and ask questions. You typically upload documentation to a secure portal, followed by interviews.

06
How long does a Specialization last?
+

Generally valid for one year. You must keep your base Solutions Partner designation active — losing the underlying designation means losing the specialization.

07
What is the Azure Expert MSP program?
+

The Azure Expert Managed Service Provider (MSP) program is a top-tier distinction for partners providing end-to-end Azure managed services. Requires an intensive audit covering people, processes, and Azure managed service capabilities. Only a small number of partners globally hold this status.

08
Are specializations mandatory?
+

Completely optional. Not required to be a Microsoft partner and not required for most incentive programs. They are an added mark of distinction.

09
Do we have to pay for a specialization?
+

No separate fee is required for specializations if you're enrolled in the Solutions Partner designation program. However, you must maintain your underlying Solutions Partner benefits package subscription.

10
Will Microsoft provide a badge for specializations?
+

Yes. Microsoft provides a digital Specialization badge / logo as an add-on to your Solutions Partner badge. Your partner profile will display both the Solutions Partner designation and the specialization credential.

Chapters I · J · K — Incentives, GTM & Partner Center

Beyond
the badge.

Sixteen questions

How designations unlock cash, how to co-sell, how to list on the Marketplace, and where every operational thing lives in Partner Center.

I·01
How do we become eligible for partner incentives?
+
  • Achieve a Solutions Partner designation (at least one) as a baseline.
  • Enroll in and sign the incentives agreement in Partner Center.
  • Meet specific program requirements for each incentive area. Microsoft's Microsoft Commerce Incentives (MCI) program covers claiming scenarios and proof of execution.
I·02
What types of incentives can partners expect?
+
  • Azure consumption incentives — based on ACR driven through PAL or CSP.
  • Modern Work incentives — based on selling or influencing M365 seats and active usage.
  • Business Apps incentives — for selling and driving adoption of Dynamics 365 and Power Platform.
  • Program-specific funding — ECIF, AMP, and PIE, increasingly tied to Advanced Specializations.
I·03
How are incentives paid out?
+

Typically paid quarterly or monthly depending on the program. You must set up payment and tax details in Partner Center.

I·04
Not transacting any deals — can we earn incentives?
+

Yes, in many cases. For Azure, if you have a PAL association and drive usage up, you may qualify for influence incentives. The key is having the corresponding Solutions Partner designation and enrolling in the incentives program.

J·01
What does "co-selling with Microsoft" mean?
+

Collaborating with Microsoft's sales teams (or other partners) to sell solutions. Forms: Microsoft field-led (a Microsoft salesperson brings you in) · Partner-led (you have a solution Microsoft promotes) · Partner-to-partner (two partners team up).

J·02
What is the Microsoft commercial marketplace?
+
  • Azure Marketplace — IT solutions (Azure VM solutions, SaaS apps, developer and data services).
  • Microsoft AppSource — Business users (Dynamics 365 add-ons, Power BI visuals, SaaS, consulting services).
  • Listing is free; transactable offers incur a standard marketplace transaction fee.
J·03
How do we list a solution on the marketplace?
+

In Partner Center → Marketplace → create a Publisher profile → create a New Offer → provide descriptions, pricing, and other details. Microsoft reviews before going live. Start with a simple "Contact me" listing if you just want lead generation.

J·04
What GTM support does Microsoft provide?
+
  • Partner Marketing Center — ready-made marketing materials, AI-powered customization.
  • Solutions Partner listing — customers find you via Microsoft partner directory, filtered by designations and specializations.
  • Co-branded materials and logo usage guidelines.
  • Events and webinars where partners can be featured.
J·05
How do we appear in the Microsoft partner directory?
+

Microsoft maintains a Partner Finder where customers search by expertise and location. Ensure your business profile in Partner Center is complete. Designations and specializations automatically display and influence search rankings.

J·06
Can we still use the old "Gold Partner" logo?
+

No. The old Gold and Silver logos are no longer valid. Partners lost associated badging in October 2022. Use only the current Solutions Partner badges.

J·07
We expected automatic leads. Is that real?
+

No. Simply joining the program does not guarantee leads or payments. Leads come from marketing efforts, co-sell programs, and the partner directory. Incentives require at least one designation, enrolling in an incentives program, and qualifying activity.

J·08
Customer moved from EA to Azure plan — does association carry over?
+

No. DPOR status does not move with them. The partner ID is not transferred. You need to set up PAL for the new subscription.

K·01
How do I access Partner Center?
+

Go to partner.microsoft.com and click on Partner Center. Sign in with the Microsoft account associated with your partner enrollment.

K·02
What roles exist in Partner Center?
+

MCAPP partner admin — manages capability scores and membership activities. Account admin — linking partner IDs via PAL/DPOR. Other roles exist for billing, referrals, and incentives management.

K·03
Where do I manage PAL / CPOR?
+

PAL is set up in the Azure Portal (not Partner Center) → "Link to a partner ID" page. CPOR claims are managed in Partner Center under the Incentives or Associations section.

K·04
Where do I see benefits and download software?
+

In Partner Center, navigate to the Benefits section. There you can find and activate product licenses, download software, manage Azure credits, and access support incidents.

Chapter M — Common Mistakes

Ten
avoidable
errors.

Field notes

The mistakes new partners make most often — every one of them is the difference between earning a designation in twelve months and missing it.

01

No PAL / CPOR Set Up

The most costly mistake. Without associations, partner capability points are not recognized. Every customer engagement must be linked to your Partner ID.

02

Confusing Benefits with Incentives

Benefits are resources part of membership. Incentives are cash payouts earned through performance. New partners sometimes assume they'll receive cash just by joining.

03

Assuming Auto-Designation at 70

For new partners not previously enrolled, you may need to actively purchase the Solutions Partner designation on the Membership offers page once you qualify.

04

Relying on One Certified Person

If that person leaves, you lose Skilling points. The scoring requires multiple distinct individuals with certifications.

05

Not Renewing Certifications

Expired certifications stop counting. Your Skilling score can drop suddenly if renewals are missed — they're free, online, and quick.

06

Wrong Partner ID for PAL

Microsoft specifies using the Associated PartnerID value shown on your partner profile. Using a different ID — global vs. location account — causes attribution issues.

07

DPOR Carry-over Assumption

When a customer migrates from EA to Azure plan, the partner ID does not transfer. You need to set up PAL for the new subscription.

08

Thinking Gold / Silver Still Exists

No more legacy competency renewals have been processed since October 2022. The old badges are no longer valid for use.

09

Skipping Business Verification

Microsoft will verify email ownership, employment, and other details — you must follow time-sensitive instructions or enrollment stalls.

10

Ignoring the SMB Path

Partners serving small businesses may qualify via the SMB track with lower thresholds but may not realize this option exists.
